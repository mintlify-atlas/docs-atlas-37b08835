Your purity score is calculated based on how many questions you checked. The fewer experiences you've had, the higher your purity score.

## The Formula

The score is calculated using this exact formula from the source code:

```typescript
const checkedCount = answers.filter(Boolean).length;
const score = Math.round(((questions.length - checkedCount) / questions.length) * 100);
```

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Count your checked items">
    Each checkbox you selected counts as one "checked" item. More checked items = more experiences.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Subtract from total">
    Take 100 (the total number of questions) and subtract your checked count.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Calculate percentage">
    Divide by 100 and multiply by 100 to get your purity percentage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Round to whole number">
    The result is rounded to the nearest whole number for your final score.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## In Plain English

- **100 questions total** — Every question counts equally
- **Each checked item LOWERS your score** — More experiences = less pure
- **Unchecked items keep your score high** — Fewer experiences = more pure
- **Maximum score is 100** (you checked nothing)
- **Minimum score is 0** (you checked everything)

<Note>
A high score means you're "pure"—you haven't experienced many of the items on the list. A low score means you've had more SJSU experiences. Neither is inherently good or bad!
</Note>

## Score Ranges

| Score Range | Rating |
| ----------- | ------ |
| 90-100 | Pure Spartan |
| 70-89 | Average Spartan |
| 50-69 | Experienced Spartan |
| 30-49 | Well-Seasoned |
| 0-29 | Beyond Cooked |

## What Happens to Your Score

After you submit your test:

1. Your score is displayed on the results page
2. Your score is **anonymously saved** to the community database
3. Your individual answers contribute to the per-question statistics
4. You can share your score with friends

<Tip>
Your score is just for fun! It's a way to reflect on your SJSU journey and see how your experiences compare to fellow Spartans.
</Tip>
